Human-Centered AI Metacognitive Learning Model (HAIML)

```

The Human-Centered AI Metacognitive Learning Model (HAIML), developed by Catheryn Reardon, PhD, is a framework for integrating artificial intelligence into learning in ways that preserve human agency, support metacognitive reflection, and promote ethical decision-making.

Within this model, students are active decision-makers who evaluate when AI supports learning, when it limits it, and how it shapes their thinking.

HAIML reframes AI in education not as a tool to complete tasks, but as a context for developing agency, metacognitive awareness, and ethical judgment.
```
