Human-Centered AI Metacognitive Learning Model (HAIML)

AI systems may remain opaque. Human judgment cannot.

The Human-Centered AI Metacognitive Learning Model (HAIML), developed by Catheryn Reardon, PhD, is a framework for learning and decision-making in an era of increasingly opaque AI systems.

As artificial intelligence becomes more powerful and less interpretable, HAIML shifts the focus from understanding the system to understanding the human thinking that surrounds it. The model preserves human agency, strengthens metacognitive awareness, and centers ethical responsibility in AI-supported environments.

Within this framework, students are not passive users of AI. They are active decision-makers who evaluate when AI supports their learning, when it limits it, and how it shapes their reasoning, confidence, and judgment.

HAIML reframes AI in education not as a tool for task completion, but as a context for developing human judgment under conditions of uncertainty.
