Human-Centered AI Metacognitive Learning Model (HAIML)

The Human-Centered AI Metacognitive Learning Model (HAIML) is a framework designed to guide how students learn with AI while maintaining human agency, critical thinking, and ethical responsibility. Rather than positioning AI as a replacement for learning, HAIML emphasizes structured engagement, reflection on how AI shapes thinking, and intentional decision-making. Students actively use AI tools, evaluate their influence on reasoning and confidence, and apply ethical frameworks to determine appropriate and responsible use.

HAIML Framework

  • Experiential Layer: Students actively engage with AI tools and learning experiences.
  • Metacognitive Layer: Students reflect on how AI influences their thinking, confidence, and decision-making.
  • Ethical Decision-Making Layer: Students evaluate responsible, transparent, and ethical AI use.

AI Use Guidelines and Ethical Framework

The following framework provides a structured, human-centered approach to AI use in coursework. Each level is designed to support student learning, transparency, and ethical decision-making while maintaining academic integrity and critical thinking.

  • Level 1: No AI use allowed
  • Level 2: AI may be used for brainstorming, idea generation, and structure
  • Level 3: AI may support feedback, revision, and clarity improvement
  • Level 4: AI-integrated creation with critical evaluation, reflection, and transparent use

Purpose

This work supports the integration of AI into higher education in a way that prioritizes critical thinking, ethical awareness, and human judgment. HAIML provides a structured yet flexible approach for instructors and students navigating AI in learning environments while keeping the human at the center of the learning process.

Research and Teaching Focus

My work focuses on human-centered AI in education, including metacognition, instructor presence, ethical AI integration, and how students experience AI-supported learning and feedback. Through both teaching and research, I explore how AI can support learning while preserving student agency, reflection, and meaningful engagement.
