Human-Centered AI Metacognitive Learning Model (HAIML)

The Human-Centered AI Metacognitive Learning Model (HAIML) is a framework designed to guide how students learn with AI while maintaining human agency, critical thinking, and ethical responsibility. Rather than positioning AI as a replacement for learning, HAIML emphasizes structured engagement, reflection on how AI shapes thinking, and intentional decision-making.

This model emerged from my work in psychology, online teaching, and AI integration in higher education. It reflects a human-centered approach in which students are not passive users of AI tools, but active learners who evaluate, question, and reflect on how those tools shape their thinking and academic work.

HAIML is especially relevant in courses where students are learning to write, reason, solve problems, and make decisions in collaboration with AI. The model supports a vision of education in which AI can extend learning opportunities while preserving human judgment, metacognitive awareness, and ethical accountability.

HAIML Framework

  • Experiential Layer: Students actively engage with AI tools, structured learning experiences, and guided practice rather than only reading about AI in theory.
  • Metacognitive Layer: Students reflect on how AI influences their thinking, confidence, revision process, and decision-making.
  • Ethical Decision-Making Layer: Students evaluate responsible, transparent, and ethical AI use through guided analysis and application.

Together, these layers create a learning environment in which AI is used to support growth rather than bypass it. The framework encourages students to remain aware of how AI contributes to their work, what they gain from using it, and what human judgment remains essential.

AI Use Guidelines and Ethical Framework

The following framework provides a structured, human-centered approach to AI use in coursework. These guidelines help students understand when AI use is not appropriate, when it may be used in limited ways, and when it can be integrated more fully with critical reflection and transparency.

  • Level 1: No AI use allowed
  • Level 2: AI for brainstorming and structure
  • Level 3: AI for feedback and revision
  • Level 4: AI-integrated creation with critical evaluation

This four-level model is designed to promote clarity, fairness, and student accountability. It also reinforces that students remain the decision-makers in their work. AI may support learning, but it does not replace critical thinking, authorship, or ethical responsibility.

Purpose

My work supports the integration of AI into higher education in a way that prioritizes critical thinking, ethical awareness, human judgment, and meaningful learning. I am especially interested in how AI can be used to strengthen learning processes rather than simply accelerate task completion.

This work is grounded in the belief that educational innovation should remain human-centered. AI should help students become more reflective, more engaged, and more thoughtful about their decisions, not less.

Research and Teaching Focus

My work focuses on human-centered AI in education, including metacognition, instructor presence, ethical AI integration, and how students experience AI-supported learning. I am particularly interested in how students think with AI, how they reflect on their use of AI, and how educators can design learning environments that preserve human agency.

My teaching and research also explore online learning, instructor social presence, critical thinking, structured AI use, and ethical decision-making in academic settings. Across these areas, I aim to develop practical models and tools that support both students and faculty as AI becomes more embedded in education.
