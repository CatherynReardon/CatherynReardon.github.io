Human-Centered AI in Education

My work focuses on integrating artificial intelligence into education in a way that preserves human agency, supports metacognitive development, and strengthens ethical decision-making. I am particularly interested in how students think with AI, how they reflect on their use of AI, and how educators design learning environments that prioritize critical thinking and responsible use.

Human-Centered AI Metacognitive Learning Model (HAIML)

The Human-Centered AI Metacognitive Learning Model (HAIML) is a framework designed to guide how students learn with AI through active engagement, reflection, and ethical evaluation. It emphasizes that students remain the decision-makers in their learning while using AI as a support, not a replacement.

HAIML brings together experiential learning, metacognitive awareness, and ethical decision-making to support deeper learning in AI-rich environments.

AI Use Guidelines

The AI Use Guidelines provide a structured, four-level framework that helps clarify when and how AI can be used in coursework. These guidelines support transparency, student accountability, and alignment with learning goals.

Rather than limiting AI use, the framework helps students engage with AI in ways that support learning, reflection, and ethical responsibility.

Faculty Implementation

Supporting faculty in integrating AI into their teaching is a central part of this work. This includes providing clear guidelines, sample assignment language, and strategies for helping students reflect on how AI shapes their thinking.

The goal is to move from uncertainty about AI to intentional, human-centered instructional design.
