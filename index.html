Human-Centered AI Metacognitive Learning Model (HAIML)

The Human-Centered AI Metacognitive Learning Model (HAIML) is designed to help students learn with AI, reflect on how AI shapes their thinking, and apply ethical decision-making in their use of emerging technologies. This framework emphasizes that students remain the decision-makers while AI serves as a tool to support learning, not replace it.

HAIML Framework

Experiential Layer

Students actively engage with AI tools and learning experiences.

Metacognitive Layer

Students reflect on how AI influences their thinking and decision-making.

Ethical Decision-Making Layer

Students evaluate responsible and ethical AI use.

Purpose

This work supports the integration of AI into higher education in a way that prioritizes critical thinking, ethical awareness, and human judgment. HAIML provides a structured yet flexible approach for instructors and students navigating AI in learning environments.

AI Use Guidelines (4-Level Model)

A structured framework to support instructional decisions about AI integration:

Level 1: No AI Use

Activities that require human skills development without AI assistance.

Level 2: AI for Brainstorming & Structure

Students use AI to explore ideas and organize their thinking.

Level 3: AI-Assisted Feedback & Revision

AI supports the revision process while students drive the creative direction.

Level 4: AI-Integrated Creation

Students create with AI while critically evaluating outputs and ethical implications.

Research

My research focuses on the effective integration of AI in educational contexts, with emphasis on:

  • Metacognitive awareness in AI-augmented learning environments
  • Ethical frameworks for responsible AI use in higher education
  • Human-centered design principles for AI educational tools
  • Student agency and decision-making with AI

I collaborate with faculty and instructors across disciplines to develop evidence-based practices for AI integration that enhance learning while maintaining critical thinking skills.

Teaching

I develop and teach courses focused on:

  • AI literacy and critical thinking for educators
  • Designing AI-integrated learning experiences
  • Ethical decision-making in technology integration
  • Metacognitive strategies for learning with AI

My teaching philosophy emphasizes active engagement, critical reflection, and practical application of concepts to classroom contexts.
