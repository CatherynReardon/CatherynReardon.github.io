About These Resources

This page brings together resources for students, educators, and academic communities who want to engage with artificial intelligence in ways that remain ethical, reflective, and human-centered. My approach emphasizes that AI should support learning rather than replace thinking.

These materials are informed by my work in human-centered AI, metacognition, online learning, and the Human-Centered AI Metacognitive Learning Model (HAIML).

AI Use Guidelines for Students

AI can support brainstorming, organization, and revision, but students must remain the primary thinkers and authors of their work. AI should not replace analysis, reflection, or academic responsibility.

Always review and verify AI-generated output. These tools can produce inaccuracies, bias, or fabricated information. Responsible use requires critical evaluation and thoughtful revision.

AI Use Guidance for Faculty

Faculty can use AI to support course design, feedback, and instructional planning. Effective integration requires clear expectations, intentional design, and continued human oversight.

Human-centered AI use should preserve student agency, promote active learning, and encourage reflection rather than dependence.

Human-in-the-Loop Principles

  • Use AI to support thinking, not replace it
  • Evaluate outputs for accuracy, bias, and relevance
  • Revise and personalize AI-generated material
  • Maintain transparency about AI use
  • Center human values such as fairness and accountability

Prompt Frameworks

Brainstorming

"Help me generate three approaches to this topic and evaluate each."

Clarification

"Explain this concept clearly while maintaining academic accuracy."

Revision

"Improve clarity and organization without changing my argument."

Critical Thinking

"What assumptions or perspectives might be missing?"

Metacognition

"Ask me questions that help me reflect on my reasoning."

Ethical AI Checklist

  • Is AI allowed for this task?
  • Am I still doing the core thinking?
  • Have I verified the accuracy?
  • Could bias or misinformation be present?
  • Am I transparent about AI use?
  • Does this support learning?

Metacognitive Reflection Prompts

  • What did I use AI for and why?
  • How did it influence my thinking?
  • What did I revise or reject?
  • What did I learn from the process?

Difficult Conversations AI Practice Tool

The Difficult Conversations AI Practice Tool is an interactive, human-centered learning experience designed to help educators, students, and professionals build confidence in navigating complex interpersonal situations. Using AI-mediated role-play, users engage in realistic scenarios that require empathy, ethical reasoning, and thoughtful communication.

This tool is grounded in human-in-the-loop learning and metacognitive reflection. It creates a structured space to practice decision-making, receive feedback, and reflect on how choices impact others.

It is particularly useful for developing skills in conflict resolution, feedback delivery, perspective-taking, and professional communication.
