Reflection Prompts for Faculty Use

These prompts are designed to move beyond simple disclosure of AI use and toward deeper metacognitive reflection. Students should be encouraged not only to describe how they used AI, but to evaluate how it shaped their thinking, supported deeper understanding, or potentially limited their engagement with the task.

Core Reflection Prompts

  • How did you use AI in this assignment?
  • What did AI help you do that you might not have done otherwise?
  • What did you revise, reject, or rethink after using AI?

Prompts Focused on Depth of Thinking

  • Did using AI help you think more deeply about the topic, or did it make parts of the task feel easier or more automatic?
  • Where did AI push your thinking further, and where might it have limited your own reasoning?
  • Did AI introduce ideas you had not considered, or did it reinforce what you were already thinking?
  • How did you decide what to keep, change, or discard from AI-generated content?

Prompts Focused on Learning and Engagement

  • How did your use of AI support your learning in this assignment?
  • In what ways might AI have reduced your need to struggle with the material, and how did that affect your learning?
  • If you had not used AI, what might have been different about your process or understanding?
  • Did using AI change how confident you felt in your work? Why or why not?

Prompts Focused on Awareness and Future Use

  • What did you learn about your own thinking process while using AI?
  • How would you use AI differently in a future assignment to support deeper learning?
  • What are the benefits and limitations of using AI for this type of task?

These prompts are most effective when used consistently across assignments. Over time, they help students develop awareness of when AI supports deeper thinking and when it may unintentionally replace it. This aligns with a human-centered approach in which students remain active participants in their own learning.
